What audiences walk away with

I don't do generic. Every talk I give is built around the room I'm actually walking into, whether that's a room of students figuring out if STEM is for them, a room of professionals trying to plan a launch without losing their minds, or a room of titleholders getting ready to answer a question they've never heard before under a spotlight. I bring the strategy and the systems thinking, but I keep it human, because the best ideas fall apart the second they stop feeling real to the people hearing them.

Whatever the topic, people leave with something they can put to use that week, not just a good feeling that fades by the parking lot.
